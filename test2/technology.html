The Polarizing Effect of Technology on American Prospects

Since technology has become increasingly integrated in every aspect of American life, extenuating from educational uses to leisure activities, technology has created a contrast between older and newer generations, from installing fears that technology will inevitably take over all facets of personal communication to liberating opportunities for technology to save lives and promote a greater appreciation towards learning.

Computer

Overview:
Although primitive computers (often without a screen) had been in use since the 1940s, the first widespread personal computer was conceived by Apple (marketed as the Apple II) in the 1980s and many of the Apple IIs were given to local schools, exposing many students and the generation to the power of mass-computing. From the limited functions of those computers to the portable-sized, increasingly-powerful computers of today, many individuals of different generations have all noticed a shift in the perspectives of youth today, including a stronger reliance on technology as well as increasingly powerful computer-learning programs.
Progression of Apple as a company and the development of personal computers.
Synthesis:
The development of computers and processing units is one of the most salient and compulsive advancements in the history of mankind – life has irrefutably changed from before individualized access to personal computers to now. This change has sparked both a wave of fundamentalism who resist the increased focus on technology and a surge of technological proponents, who see computers as the key a global, interconnected world. According to Mr. Scott Deruiter, as an AP CS teacher, he has witnessed the change in programming languages firsthand, having taken his first programming course at a local college. The newfound power in computer language, he says, is going to become the future of man, as everyday operations are becoming more and more reliant on this technology. However, Mr. Deruiter also warns of the “technological wash” that accompanies the increased usage of computers – citing that “communicating that way is so easy that it takes away from the student’s ability to communicate with their instructors, adults, and even amongst one another.”

​Mr. Deruiter is not alone in his concern for the next generation and its reliance on computers, although this is by no means an unwanted development. Mrs. Irene Wong has personally witnessed firsthand how non-educational uses of computers, such as “playing computer games when kids should be outside… surfing the web and YouTube videos when kids should be reading a book… spending more time on the computer than with real people”, can be destructive to the wellbeing of an individual. Mr. Preston Wong compares this phenomenon of computers to other critically-received works in the past, such as comic books or plays, stating that “each generation has its own share of concerns the previous generations oppose – this is just a naturally part of history and only time will tell whether any invention has a long-lasting positive or negative impact on society”. Regardless of whether computers are perceived as a positive, negative, or neutral force, they are here to stay and improper technology use will continue to be a hotly contended topic amongst different generations.

Cell Phone

Overview:
Similar to the computer, modern, portable cell phones derived their first uses with a microprocessor conceived from the technological advances of the late 20th century. The widespread usage of these cell phones allowed for increased communication abroad, resulting in not only a decline in certain means of transportation, but also a reduction of in-person communication, a phenomenon many older generations decry. The abundance of cell phones also resulted the curbing of more traditional forms of long-distance communication, such as telegrams and postal-office mail.
Overview of the history of a mobile phone.
Synthesis:
Portable communication devices, most notably cell phones, were primary cause for the depreciation of postal mail and other forms of communication. The capability to lead a conversation with an individual halfway around the world allots the individual power that would not have been dreamed of even just 50 years ago. However, as more individuals rely on cell phones and their increasingly addictive integrations and capabilities, many older generations begin to fear the results of a deduction in face-to-face communication – the creation of a “zombie-like class of human beings” according to Mrs. Irene Wong. In addition, the learning gap to use these new cell phones is increasingly difficult, as Mrs. Mia Onodera finds, it is no wonder that “kids cannot focus [as much on] their studies [if they have] so many technological functions to master”.

​The difficulty of using these devices, however, overshadows the way cell phones have nearly completely driven traditional forms of communication of the market. Mr. Albert Boledo recalls that during his youth, “the only way I could talk to my relatives in South America was through ‘snail mail’... that method was slow and inefficient… [long-distance] calling and emails nowadays allows us to do this much quicker and cheaper”. It is clear that cell phones had an indelible effect on the American people, as cell phones perceived in a broad context has helped unified the nation, but in a more microscopic level, has also contributed to the divide between generations, as more young people are capable of utilizing these phones compared to older generations.

Big Data  & Internet

Overview:
Initially developed as a method of mass-communication amongst the US Army by DARPA, the internet’s capabilities to record data and connect people from across all distances and age groups quickly captivates the attention of American society. Especially in the late 20th century, the internet experienced a proliferation of users, as nations all around the world adopted the internet as a primary form of widespread data-sharing. Complementing the growth of the internet and often serving as the impetus, copious amounts of data were transmitted across the internet, often at the cost of publicizing private networks of data via hackers as well as benefiting society through improved health procedures through the newly-garnered ability to analyze genomic sequences.
Overview of the history of the internet.
Synthesis:
The proliferation of computer users marked the need for an interconnected network of information, in which data can be transmitted from one computer to the next, and thus this web of interconnected data transmission became known as the internet. Mr. Preston Wong gathers that the internet is amongst the most impressionable tools of his generation – that “data sharing and communication has never been so easy as [it is] now. I can literally go to my favorite search engine and search up anything you say right now… and the results will show me exactly what you mean if not more”. Mr. Wong also expresses concerns that the untamed nature of the internet, while is a valuable asset to the freedom of expression upheld by the American Constitution, propagates destructive dissent and exclusive access to certain data, citing that over 90% of the internet is the “dark web”, data not indexed by search engines and only accessible to authorized users.

​Mr. Albert Boledo also sees the negativity spread online as an unfortunate yet not unexpected circumstance of the internet – he asserts that “no matter what the [medium to] communicate with each other is, the root problem of anonymity promotes more radical thoughts and [is the reason] why there is so much negativity on the internet”. Still, big data has facilitated a lot of medical research, particularly in the genomics sector, which would otherwise have not been possible. According to Mrs. Irene Wong, her friend, who is a geneticist, cites how the internet with its publicly available database of DNA information, is the most pertinent reason for the success of DNA therapy and the collaborative nature of international genome projects. Regardless of personal affiliation, it is clear that big data and internet are polarizing topics which are here to stay – they can not only serve as a rallying point for society, but the crisis point for society’s values and virtues as well.
