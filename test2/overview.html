Generation-Defining...

﻿﻿History is, by all accounts, more than the study of events as they happen – it incorporates elements of perspective and differing approaches to analyzing the reactions to the events as they happen. Historical perspective and by extension personal preferences play a key role in an individual’s response to the different events in history. However, one theme is common despite the categorical differences amongst these individuals – certain events the nation experienced as a whole will be forever cemented in their minds. Every aspect and surrounding details of one of these events will be nearly perfectly recalled. As an individual born into the age of massive data, understanding the events and responses which unified the nation is vital to not only the preservation of American culture and perspectives, but also to better understand the differences elicited amongst different generations simply due to the events they experienced.
